abstract: >-
 Against the backdrop of smart healthcare shifting from data-driven development to knowledge service-driven development, this study draws on triadic world theory and knowledge organization theory to construct a componentized knowledge service pattern for smart healthcare driven by large language models (LLMs) agents. Medical knowledge resources are not static collections of data, but are generated through interactions among human society, physical world, and digital space. These resources need to be integrated through subject connection, modality alignment, semantic association, and temporal continuity, before being processed through structured representation, semantic enrichment, rule-based organization, and vectorization into knowledge components that can be understood, invoked, and governable by agents. This study positions LLMs agents as central control system for knowledge services and reveals how they support knowledge scheduling, evidence organization, component invocation, and feedback optimization around medical tasks, thereby supporting continuous knowledge services across pre-diagnosis, diagnosis, and post-diagnosis stages. The case of the collaborative “Lingshan-Sushan” dual-model system demonstrates the explanatory value of the proposed model in traditional Chinese medicine diagnosis and treatment, showing how traditional Chinese medicine knowledge resources can be transformed into componentized knowledge service capabilities through resource construction, task division, and agent collaboration. This study provides theoretical insights for the organization of smart healthcare knowledge resources and the innovation of healthcare service pattern.
